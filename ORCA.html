The Next Generation of ORCA


Reimagining Seattle Public Transportation

overview projects projects

ORCA, Puget Sound’s inter-agency public transit system, has had a stagnant 60% adoption rate for years despite the best efforts of transit agencies. It has been demonstrated in other cities, namely London, that eliminating cash payment can increase the efficiency of public transit by up to 30%. For our project, we took on the challenge of reducing cash payment in Seattle, specifically for event attendees as a type of infrequent rider.

We began by performing user research and usability testing on the TransitGO mobile ticketing application. We identified some key functionality that users expect in a mobile transit service as well as came to the conclusion that mobile ticketing does not meet the needs of our users.

We identified next gen ORCA as the best reasonable solution and dedicated our project to designing the best mobile experience for ORCA users we could based on our research. ORCA pay, our final application, is designed to fit into the next gen ORCA system that is planned to be rolled out in 2021. Allowing users to instantly manage several ORCA accounts at once as well as use their phone as a credential to pay for train, light rail, bus, streetcar, and water taxi fare.

This is an brief overview of the process and the deliverables created for our 2019 HCDE capstone project. The project was a collaboration of our team and our sponsors, the CoMotion Mobility Innovation Center, King County Metro, Sound Transit, along with Challenge Seattle, the Seattle Department of Transportation and the Washington State Department of Transportation. Capstone team 20 (Infrequent Riders: Event Attendees) includes Ryan Zuzelski, Zoe Escalona, Zach Thomas and myself, Sierramatice Karras.

My primary responsibilities included the application design and the interactive prototype as well as having a major hand in the planning and implementation phases of the research and evaluation stages of the project and the design work for the TransitGO recommendations.

The goal we were given by our sponsors was to

reduce cash fare payments and increase a stagnant 60% ORCA adoption rate.

We initially planned to approach this through a redesign of the existing TransitGO application, but the results of our research led us in a different direction.

Mobility Innovation Center

The Mobility Innovation Center (MIC) is sponsored by Challenge Seattle and the University of Washington to “tackle specific transportation challenges, using applied research and experimentation”. We were one of the teams that they set to “attack regional mobility problems, develop new technologies, apply system-level thinking, and bring new innovations to our regional transportation system”. Our main point of contact with the MIC was with Gaia Borgias, who helped coordinate the biweekly meetings between us and representatives of Sound Transit and King County Metro, as well coordinating as our mid-project and final presentations of to our sponsors.

Sound Transit

Sound Transit is a regional transit organization that services Pierce, King and Snohomish Counties. They operate the Link light rail, Sounder trains, ST Express Bus, Tacoma Link light rail, and Bus Rapid Transit. Our primary point of contact here was Michael Berman, who attended our biweekly meetings via teleconference.

King County Metro

King County Metro is responsible for providing a wide variety of transportation types, including the region’s largest bus network, vanpools, paratransit, as well as operating some Sound Transit services and the Seattle Streetcar. During our project our primary point of contact was Matt Hansen who attended our biweekly meetings.

Challenge Seattle

Challenge Seattle is a board made up of CEOs from 17 of the region’s largest employers. Former Washington Governor Christine Gregoire is the CEO of the board which includes the CEOs of companies like Alaska Airline, Amazon and Boeing. We worked with them mostly through MIC and our presentation to their director of programs.

Seattle Department of Transportation

Seattle Department of Transportation aims to deliver a high-quality transportation system for the city. We didn’t directly work with the Seattle Department of Transportation but their goals align with the goals of our project.

Washington State Department of Transportation

The Washington State Department of Transportation manages thousands of miles of roads and the biggest ferry system in the United States. We didn’t directly work with the Washington State Department of Transportation but their goals align with the goals of our project.

Capstone Open House

The final presentation we had of our project was at the 2019 HCDE Open House. This was a joint presentation of all the HCDE bachelors and masters capstone projects from the 2018-2018 academic year. For this presentation we created a poster and business cards with access to the interactive prototype and the promotion video as well as having a phone running the interactive prototype and a laptop running the promotional video for people to view. Additional things we did to bring in more context of our project was having small light rails on the table and wearing the King County Metro vests we were given to wear at the while conducting our on the ground interviews and usability studies.

I spoke to many open house attendees and judges, giving an elevator pitch version of our process and deliverables, as well answering any questions. All of our efforts for our project and at the open house resulted our team winning the people’s choice award.
